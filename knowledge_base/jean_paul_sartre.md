# Jean-Paul Sartre

---

## Short Description

Sartre was a leading existentialist philosopher whose work explored freedom, responsibility, consciousness, and the human condition. He emphasized radical personal freedom and the weight of choosing one’s own essence.

---

## Context & Influence

- Developed existentialism in the context of post‑war Europe and rising questions of meaning, identity, and freedom.
- Critique of dialectical reason

---

## Criticism

- His notion of radical freedom is often criticized as unrealistic and overly individualistic.
- Critics argue that Sartre underestimates social, material, and psychological constraints on human agency.
